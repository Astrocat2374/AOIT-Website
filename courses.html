Programming

Computer Programming A (Coding) EXS1400 0.5 Credit Open to: 10-12
Computer Programming B (Coding) EXS1500 0.5 Credit Open to: 10-12

These courses covers HTML, JavaScript, and CSS. Students will learn to combine these markup and scripting languages to create interactive websites. Emphasis will be placed on writing efficient code that is also easy to understand. Barcode Location - Mrs. Takara, J-9

A+

A+ Certification 1: Intro to Computer TIU5510 0.5 credit Open to:10-12
A+ Certification 2: Computer Systems TIU5511 0.5 credit Open to: 10-12

This high school course discusses the history of computer systems, the hardware content of a computer and their mechanics for constructing a computer system. The topics covered will include safety, basic electronics, power supply, chipsets, motherboards, Pentium processors, and USB Standards. An internship program will be recommended for students. Barcode Location - Mrs. Takara, J-9



A+ Certification 3: Intro to Computer TIN5512 0.5 Credit Open to: 10-12
A+ Certification 4: Computer Systems TIN5513 0.5 Credit Open to: 10-12

A+ Certification course covers the installation, configuration, maintenance, and troubleshooting of other network operation systems. The topics covered include featrues and navigation of user interface, implementation guideliens, installation procedures, upgrade concers, hardware management, softare management, user management, and security management. An internship program will be recommended for students. A+ Certification prepares the students with a formal internship program. Students will have instruction and hands-on experience related to career fields in the computer industry. Topics will also include job outlook, interview skills, workplace ethics, customer satisfaction, and studen'ts ability to demonstrate positive work habits. Barcode Location - Mrs. Takara, J-9

Cyber Security

Cyber Security TIN5520 1 Credit Open to: 11-12

Network Security builds upon the knowledge learned from the IET Core and Network and LAN Fundamentals and has students apply that knowledge into securing system networks. Through this course, students will identify vulnerabilites and attack mechanisms to a network on various platforms and use intrustion detection systems and other methods to mitigage security risks. Emphasis will be placed on the application of skills in detection and the utilization of stragegies to combat identified threats. Barcode Location - Mrs. Takara, J-9

CISCO Networking

Networking Fundamentals & Local Area Networks TIU5713 1 Credit Open to: 10-12

CISCO 1 is a course designed to provide studnets with classroom and laboratory experience in current and emerging networking technology that will empower them to enter employment and/or further education and training in the computer-networking field. Instructions includes, but is not limited to, safety, networking, networking terminiology and protocols, network standards, LAN's, WAN's, OSI models, cabling, cabling tools, routers, router programming, star topology, IP addressing, and network standards. Particular emphasis is given to the use of decision-making and problem-solving techniques in applying science, mathematics, communication and social studies concepts to solve networking problems. In addition, instruction and training are provided in the proper care, maintenance and use of networking software, tools and equipment. Barcode Location - Mrs. Takara, J-9



Wide Area Networks and Networking Architectures TIN5716 1 Credit Open to: 11-12

CISCO 2 is a course designed to provide students with advanced classroom and laboratory experience in current and emerging networking technology that will empower students to enter employment and/or further education and training in the computer networking field. Instructions includes, but is not limited to advance studies in safety, networking, networking terminology and protocols, network standards, LAN's, WAN's, OSI models, Ethernet, Token Ring, Fiber distribution Interface, TCP/IP Addressing Protocol, Dynamic Routing, Routing, and the Network Administrator's role and function. Particular emphasis is given to the use of decision-making and problem-solving techniques in applying science, mathematics, communication and social studies concepts to solve networking proglems. In addition, instruction and training are provided in the proper care, maintenance and use of networking software, tools and equipment and all local, state, and federal safety, building and environmental codes and regualtions. Barcode Location - Mrs. Takara, J-9

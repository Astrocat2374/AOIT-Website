AOIT Courses

Programming

Computer Programming A (Coding) EXS1400 0.5 Credit Open to: 10-12
Computer Programming B (Coding) EXS1500 0.5 Credit Open to: 10-12

This course covers the essential building blocks of web development needed to create eye catching interactive web applications and games. Students will gain an in-depth knowledge of modern tools in the industry today: Hypertext Markup Language (HTML), Cascading Style Sheets (CSS), and JavaScript, as well as getting started with the command line and GitHub.This course will focus on the fundamentals of programming in JavaScript. Students will learn what makes for good app designs, game designs, and how to apply the rapid protoype process. At the end of this course, students will have built a portfolio of web applicatins and games to showcase.


Coding Year II

Students will dive deep into the Engineering Design Process and Rapid Protoyping. Students will learn a variety of back end web topics including handling user input, producing templated output, storing information in databases and data stores, and building applications with secure user information. Upon completion of this course, students will be able to create complex, interactive web applications from scratch and be able to deploy them to a cloud-based hosting provider. Students will understand web architecture, including databases, and how web or mobile applications will interact with APIs. Students will also understand the role that developers play on an overall product team to prepare for a transition into a work environemt. Studenss will also have the skills to becaome a Certified Amazon Web Services Developer.

A+

A+ Certification 1: Intro to Computer TIU5510 0.5 credit Open to:10-12
A+ Certification 2: Computer Systems TIU5511 0.5 credit Open to: 10-12

This high school course discusses the history of computer systems, the hardware content of a computer and their mechanics for constructing a computer system. The topics covered will include safety, basic electronics, power supply, chipsets, motherboards, Pentium processors, and USB Standards. An internship program will be recommended for students.


A+ Certification 3: Intro to Computer TIN5512 0.5 Credit Open to: 10-12
A+ Certification 4: Computer Systems TIN5513 0.5 Credit Open to: 10-12

A+ Certification course covers the installation, configuration, maintenance, and troubleshooting of other network operation systems. The topics covered include featrues and navigation of user interface, implementation guideliens, installation procedures, upgrade concers, hardware management, softare management, user management, and security management. An internship program will be recommended for students. A+ Certification prepares the students with a formal internship program. Students will have instruction and hands-on experience related to career fields in the computer industry. Topics will also include job outlook, interview skills, workplace ethics, customer satisfaction, and studen'ts ability to demonstrate positive work habits.

Cyber Security

Cyber Security TIN5520 1 Credit Open to: 11-12

Network Security builds upon the knowledge learned from the IET Core and Network and LAN Fundamentals and has students apply that knowledge into securing system networks. Through this course, students will identify vulnerabilites and attack mechanisms to a network on various platforms and use intrustion detection systems and other methods to mitigage security risks. Emphasis will be placed on the application of skills in detection and the utilization of stragegies to combat identified threats.

CISCO Networking

Networking Fundamentals & Local Area Networks TIU5713 1 Credit Open to: 10-12

CISCO 1 is a course designed to provide students with classroom and laboratory experience in current and emerging networking technology that will empower them to enter employment and/or further education and training in the computer-networking field. Instructions includes, but is not limited to, safety, networking, networking terminiology and protocols, network standards, LAN's, WAN's, OSI models, cabling, cabling tools, routers, router programming, star topology, IP addressing, and network standards. Particular emphasis is given to the use of decision-making and problem-solving techniques in applying science, mathematics, communication and social studies concepts to solve networking problems. In addition, instruction and training are provided in the proper care, maintenance and use of networking software, tools and equipment.



Wide Area Networks and Networking Architectures TIN5716 1 Credit Open to: 11-12

CISCO 2 is a course designed to provide students with advanced classroom and laboratory experience in current and emerging networking technology that will empower students to enter employment and/or further education and training in the computer networking field. Instructions includes, but is not limited to advance studies in safety, networking, networking terminology and protocols, network standards, LAN's, WAN's, OSI models, Ethernet, Token Ring, Fiber distribution Interface, TCP/IP Addressing Protocol, Dynamic Routing, Routing, and the Network Administrator's role and function. Particular emphasis is given to the use of decision-making and problem-solving techniques in applying science, mathematics, communication and social studies concepts to solve networking proglems. In addition, instruction and training are provided in the proper care, maintenance and use of networking software, tools and equipment and all local, state, and federal safety, building and environmental codes and regualtions.
